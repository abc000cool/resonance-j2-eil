Formation-flying missions in low Earth orbit (LEO) --- PRISMA
\cite{damico2012prisma}, TanDEM-X \cite{montenbruck2008tandemx}, GRACE-FO
\cite{kornfeld2019gracefo}, NASA Starling \cite{kruger2025starling}, and the
upcoming VISORS telescope \cite{guffanti2023visors} --- must continuously
counteract the differential secular drift induced by the Earth-oblateness
$J_2$ harmonic. The controller that performs this formation-keeping never
sees the true relative state: it acts on the estimate produced by a relative
navigation filter, so the achievable delta-V budget is a joint function of
the navigation accuracy, the controller family, and the fidelity of the
relative-motion model used for prediction.

The single most-cited systematic treatment of this coupling, Koenig \&
D'Amico \cite{koenig2018tradeoffs}, characterizes the trade in an LTI,
near-circular, LQR-only setting with the estimator abstracted as a static
covariance floor. This paper extends that analysis along five axes:
(i) three controllers instead of one; (ii) three relative-motion STMs
including the modern quasi-nonsingular ROE STM
\cite{koenig2017stm}; (iii) three realistic measurement architectures with
actual filters running in the loop, not a static covariance; (iv) a Pareto
\emph{surface} instead of a single trade curve; and (v) an explicit
characterization of the regime where the certainty-equivalence assumption
breaks down.
